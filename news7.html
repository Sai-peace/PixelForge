Cyberpunk 2077: Phantom Liberty Breaks Records

CD Projekt Red redeems itself with a masterclass in narrative and design.

A Triumphant Redemption Story

After a rocky launch in 2020, few expected Cyberpunk 2077 to make such a massive comeback. Yet, with the 2025 release of its expansion Phantom Liberty, CD Projekt Red has achieved exactly that — earning critical acclaim, record-breaking sales, and renewed respect from gamers worldwide.

Set in the shadowy district of Dogtown, Phantom Liberty introduces new characters, moral dilemmas, and espionage-driven storytelling that push the boundaries of immersive world-building. The return of Keanu Reeves as Johnny Silverhand, alongside new faces like Idris Elba’s Solomon Reed, gave the expansion cinematic depth rarely seen in modern games.

The expansion’s launch shattered records, peaking at over 5 million players within the first week — a testament to how far the game’s reputation has evolved. Performance improvements and rebuilt police AI systems finally allowed players to experience Night City as it was originally envisioned.

Fans and critics have praised CD Projekt Red’s dedication, calling Phantom Liberty “a masterclass in redemption storytelling.” The DLC not only expands gameplay but also reshapes the main story’s conclusion, adding emotional resonance and new choices that alter the fate of both V and Night City.

With the success of Phantom Liberty, CD Projekt Red confirmed that the upcoming sequel, codenamed Project Orion, is already in early development. If this expansion is any indication, the future of Cyberpunk looks brighter — and more neon-lit — than ever.
